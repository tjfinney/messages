Poor man's bread

Bread and wine What Christians call communion or the Lord’s supper started off as a Passover meal that Jesus ate with his disciples on the night before he went to the cross. During communion we eat something like bread and drink something like wine. Communion is a symbolic meal, and tonight we will look at the symbolism of the bread. Next month I hope to look at the symbolism of the wine.

Beautiful feet

Tonight we will look at something from Luke’s Gospel, which tells us about what happened once when Jesus was invited to a meal. Here is how it starts: One of the Pharisees invited him to a meal. When he arrived at the Pharisee’s house and took his place at table, a woman came in, who had a bad name in the town. She had heard he was dining with the Pharisee and had brought with her an alabaster jar of ointment.
